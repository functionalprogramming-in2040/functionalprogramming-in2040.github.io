    About these pages

    The pages here collect information in connection with the lecture IN2040 (functional programming). Information for the lecture is available at different pages on the web. The purpose of the pages here (resp. referenced from here) is twofold: 1) to collect in one place relevant information but also 2) to give additional, (non-pensum) background information that may be interesting.

    Charting out material and information in connection with IN2040

    A good part of the official information is kept resp. uploaded at the pages https://www.uio.no/studier/emner/matnat/ifi/IN2040/, resp. https://www.uio.no/studier/emner/matnat/ifi/IN2040/h23 for the autumn 2023 semester.

    The pages here organize partly the same information. I.e., the PDFs for the lectures are referenced here as well as under the official page https://www.uio.no/studier/emner/matnat/ifi/IN2040/. Not all information is duplicated resp. referenced here as well, especially not ``beskjeder’’ or official regulations and information concerning the exams.

    Indeed, there is quite a lot of information maintained internally and to be published externally, with ``sources’’ kept in various places, (kept at different git repositories, hosted at different web-addresses). It’s difficult to keep all that coherent. Though in principle, all of that info could be kept at vortex (and to a good extent it is, especially all pensum information), vortex is cumbersome to fill with content (basically filling in web-forms) and furthermore can be quite a straightjacket. On top of that, one has to repeat the exercise year after year (and treat every piece of information individually, so one cannot bulk-upload all relevant information).

    Additional peripheral information

    Apart from charting and organizing links to official information, this site also contains additional information, in the form of blog-posts. This information is not pensum information, just trying to shed light on aspects or topics is issues covered in the lecture, in connection with functional programming. Those posts are collected at here.
